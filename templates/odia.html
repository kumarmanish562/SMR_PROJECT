Language Differs. But Translation Unites.

Oriya is an Indian language belonging to the Indo-Aryan branch of the Indo-European language family. It is the predominant language of the Indian state of Odisha, where native speakers comprise 80% of the population. It is also spoken in parts of West Bengal, Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, and Andhra Pradesh. Oriya is one of the many official languages in India, being the official language of Odisha and the second official language of Jharkhand.

There are 33 million native Oriya language speakers in India.
