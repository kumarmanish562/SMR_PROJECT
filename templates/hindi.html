Language Differs. But Translation Unites.

According to the 2001 census, Hindi is spoken by about 422 million people in India or people who have Hindi as their native language. This includes people who may speak various dialects of Hindi and not just Hindustani which is the root.

So out of the total population 1,028,610,328 in 2001, about 41.1% were Hindi speakers mostly concentrated in Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Chhattisgarh, Himachal Pradesh, Chandigarh, Bihar, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Haryana, and Rajasthan.

Varieties of Hindi spoken in India include Rajasthani, Braj Bhasha, Haryanvi, Bundeli, Kannauji, Hindustani, Awadhi, Bagheli, Bhojpuri, Magahi, and Chhattisgarhi. By virtue of its being a lingua franca, Hindi has also developed regional dialects such as Bambaiya Hindi in Mumbai.

Hindi is widely taught both as a primary language and language of instruction, and as a second tongue in most states.

Source: Wikipedia.

Other Important Facts about Hindi Language:

  • ✅ Hindi is the 4th most spoken language in the world
  • ✅ Apart from India, Hindi is also spoken in Nepal, Thailand, Fiji, Pakistan, Suriname, and Guyana.
  • ✅ There are more than 1200 million people worldwide knowing Hindi. This figure is higher than Mandarin Chinese.
  • ✅ Hindi is taught in 176 of the World’s Universities
  • ✅ By 2021, an expected 201 million Hindi users—38% of the Indian internet user base—will be online, according to the KPMG-Google study.
