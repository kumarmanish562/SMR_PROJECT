Our Specialization

The medical sector is always in a constant development stage. Every day, new drugs and medical devices are introduced. The medical industry is also one of the highest growth industries in the entire world. It is divided into several sectors, including life sciences, biotechnology, pharmaceuticals, services, and more. To communicate with foreign patients, medical professionals need to opt for medical translation services.

Professional medical translation services involve the translation of various medical documents such as drug data sheets, training materials for healthcare, patient records, prescriptions, medical history, and more.

The healthcare market is expected to increase three-fold to Rs 8.6 trillion by 2022. (ibef.org)

Importance of Accuracy in Medical Document Translations

  • Incorrect Translation Can Lead to Legal Risks: The medical translator must ensure that the imported medication is provided with accurate information, including dosage forms, side effects, manufacturing date, expiry date, and more. Inaccurate translations can lead to legal risks.
  • Errors Are Unacceptable: Medical document translation requires precision. Incorrect words or omitted information can lead to serious complications, making it critical to ensure there is no room for error.
  • Bad Translation Can Cause Death: Relying on family members for interpretation can result in fatal errors. Even a small mistake can cost lives, so it is crucial to hire a professional medical translation service provider.

Medical Translation Process

  • Extracting text from the source format
  • Translating text to the target language
  • Editing by a separate person
  • Publishing the translation in the original format
  • Proofreading to ensure the formatted translation is perfect
  • Reviewing in-country by a native-speaking expert to ensure the translation meets all requirements
