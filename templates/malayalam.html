Language Differs. But Translation Unites.

Malayalam is a Dravidian language spoken prominently in the state of Kerala in India. It is also spoken in Lakshadweep and Mahe. It is one of the 22 official languages of India. Malayalam is the official language in the State of Kerala, Lakshadweep and Mahe District in the Union territory of Puducherry. There were a total of 38 million native speakers of Malayalam language speakers in India as per census 2011.

A large number of Malayalees have migrated to Middle East, United States, and Europe. Malayalam is a classical language. Malayalam incorporated many words from Sanskrit language. Malayalam most likely originated from Tamil in the 6th century.

Other Important Facts about Malayalam Language:

  • ✅ Malayalam is one of the 22 official languages of India.
  • ✅ Malayalam is the official language of Kerala, Lakshadweep, and Mahe.
  • ✅ The total number of Malayalam speakers in India is over 38 million, according to the 2011 census.
  • ✅ Malayalam is considered a classical language of India.
  • ✅ A significant number of Malayalees have migrated to various countries across the world.
