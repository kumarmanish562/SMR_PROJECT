The marketing world is ever-changing – trends and technology keep updating. With global marketing advancement, your business also needs to stay up to date with new concepts and industry trends. To educate your customers about the latest technology and trends, it is essential to speak in their language. Here arises the need for marketing translation services.

Marketing translation is the process of translating marketing materials, taking into consideration the cultural preferences of the target audience. A marketing translator has knowledge of different writing styles and takes into account the specific culture of the target locale while performing translations.

Marketing Translation - Things You Should Know

  • Local Cultural Context: Marketing translation must have local appeal. If the marketing translator is from the target country, they will understand the local culture easily. They will be aware of images and expressions that may not be acceptable in the target culture. This helps in adapting the marketing content appropriately.
  • Tricky Slogans: Slogans are carefully crafted for repetition so that they are memorable. However, slogans can be tricky to translate, and require special attention and care to maintain their effectiveness across different markets.
  • Tone and Style: Tone and style are important elements in marketing translations. It’s vital to understand the target audience before beginning the translation process, ensuring the translation resonates with the audience through either a formal or informal approach.
  • Voice and Value of the Brand: Maintaining the voice of the brand while adapting the content to local culture is key. A translator must choose the right words to convey the same message, while staying true to the brand’s identity.
  • Colors and Imagery: Colors and imagery play a significant role in brand identity. Different cultures associate various colors with specific meanings (e.g., white is associated with peace in the West, but in parts of Asia, it may be seen as a color of misfortune). A translator must consider these cultural nuances when adapting the content.
