Language Differs. But Translation Unites.

Telugu is a Dravidian language and is the only language other than Hindi and Bengali that is spoken prominently in more than one Indian state, namely Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. It is also a significant minority language in Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, Puducherry, Andaman and Nicobar Islands in India and in Sri Lanka. Telugu is spoken by significant minorities in the United States, Australia, Canada, New Zealand, Bahrain, Fiji, Malaysia, Ireland, South Africa, Trinidad and Tobago, United Arab Emirates, and the United Kingdom. Telugu is the third most spoken language after Hindi and Bengali in the Indian Sub-continent. There are a total of 75 million native speakers of Telugu language in India and worldwide.
